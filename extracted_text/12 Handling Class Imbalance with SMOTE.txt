What is Class Imbalance?
A dataset where one class (the majority) vastly outnumbers another (the minority) is said to be class-
imbalanced. In many real-world classification problems, one class is far more common than another.
вАҐ Fraud detection: 0.1% of transactions are fraudulent
вАҐ Medical diagnosis: 1вАУ5% of patients have a rare disease
вАҐ Spam filtering: Spam may be a small fraction of all email
вАҐ Network intrusion detection: Malicious traffic is a tiny fraction of all network packets.
вАҐ Defect detection in manufacturing: Most manufactured items are defect-free; defective cases may be 
<0.5%.
вАҐ Wildlife conservation / species detection: Camera traps show animals in only a small fraction of images; 
rare species appear in <1%.
вАҐ Earthquake early warning: True seismic events are extremely rare compared to non-events in continuous 
sensor data.
вАҐ Credit default prediction: Defaults occur in only a small % of loans.
вАҐ Coastal water quality monitoring: Hypoxic events (dissolved oxygen вЙ§2.0 mg/L) are rare relative to normal 
(normoxic) conditions, creating a highly imbalanced dataset for binary classification.
